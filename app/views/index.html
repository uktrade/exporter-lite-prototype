Version 1
Tested between 19 November to 4 December 2019

View prototype

View prototype (no JavaScript)

What we learned

  • Some variation among exporters depending on how their trade control functions were organised
  • Good to have compliance and annual returns section lifted up hierarchy so they appear at the same level as applying for licences and so on
  • Labelling of all sections understood in terms of what users would find in those sections, however:
    • "Goods and services" was not recognised, particularly "services" as participants could not relate things being licensable to services
    • Persistent issue (as encountered elsewhere) about "Goods" not being perceived to include technology and software (exporters typically see "goods" as hardware rather than as an all-encompassing term for licensable things being exported)
    • Some participants did not know what PV gradings were as they had never applied for one (research inference is that if they had to apply for a PV grading, then this lack of familiarity would not have been encountered)
  • "Your account" section reveal concept was a familiar design pattern to participants
  • Good to have account management separated from the other sections
  • Some (minor) terminology points raised - there is organisation / team administration in there as well as user's own account so at odds with conventions elsewhere (where "Your account" is exclusively for self-servicing on account-related matters) / "Sites" not necessarily understood as locations
  • Notifications alerts (red dot with number in) not sometimes understood as relating to notifications exclusively. For some participants, the number in the dot was inferred to mean number of things in that section, for example number of applications in progress ("Check progress" notifications)
  • One participant was looking for additional graphics to reinforce what the sections were - for example having a team icon alongside "Team settings" in Your account

Goods database

Version 1
Tested between 19 November to 4 December 2019

View prototype

What we learned

On the 'Goods and services' landing page:

  • Preference to have part number adjacent to name (exporters make sense of which product is which by one of the name, part number or combination of part number)
  • For many exporters, the CLC will be the same for all their goods owing to the nature of their activities, so it is not that important for sense making

For 'Good name 1 to Good name 4' examples:

  • Some of the language was not immediately understood, especially "under review" and "be verified" in the grey boxes on the right
  • The verified status indicator (green "Verified" alongside product name) was understood and valued
  • Good name 4 - Users responded positively to having the ability to see which applications had included a specific product however questions were raised about what these applications (that is, current?, decided?) and also how these applications were chronicled (that is, this year?, ever?)

Version 1

What we learned

For the Products section:

  • Difficult to scan
  • Not possible to check easily for duplicates
  • Not possible to check easily for missing items
  • Not possible to check easily for incorrectly added items
  • Not possible to compare items easily
  • Not possible to edit the items / section
Version 2
Tested between 19 November to 4 December 2019

View prototype

View prototype (version 2.1)

What we learned

  • Positive response as it enables exporters to preview application prior to submission
  • Perceived to be a user friendly improvement on current application preview in SPIRE
  • Ability to go back to specific part of the application welcomed (although users should then be able to return to the 'Check your answers' page rather than have to navigate sequentially through the application form as is the current behaviour in SPIRE)
  • Having goods listed on separate page prompted various responses:
    • On one hand, always have goods listed separately regardless of number of goods in application to enforce consistency
    • On the other, if less than n goods in application, then those goods should be listed within the 'Check your answers' page (differing preferences for what n should be, ranging between 3 and 10)
  • One other suggestion was to have the ability to make changes within the 'Check your answers' page rather than returning to the specific page in the application form
  • This page is also preferred to the 'Task list' patterm for checking status of the application preparation
  • One terminology issue raised - "Manage" for call to action to check goods information did not make it clear what a user could do on the goods listing page
